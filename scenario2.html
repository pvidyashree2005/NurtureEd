Puberty Awareness (Boys & Girls)

You begin to notice several changes in your body as you grow older. Your voice may be changing, your height is increasing rapidly, or you experience mood swings that you don’t fully understand.

These changes make you feel confused and sometimes embarrassed, especially when you compare yourself with others. You may hesitate to talk about it, thinking others might judge you or that something is wrong. Because of this, you start overthinking and feel stressed. This situation is common during growing years, but lack of awareness and communication can make it feel more difficult than it actually is.

Review & Insights

B. Talk to a trusted adult or teacher to understand it

Talking to a trusted adult or teacher is the best choice because they can guide you correctly. They help you understand that these physical and emotional changes are natural. This support reduces confusion, builds confidence, and ensures you don’t feel alone while going through this important stage of development.

A.Ignore the changes and feel stressed

Ignoring these changes may seem easier, but it increases confusion and stress over time. Without proper understanding, you might develop unnecessary fear or anxiety. Avoiding the situation does not solve it; instead, it prevents you from learning that these changes are normal and part of growing up.

C. Believe something is wrong with you

Thinking something is wrong with you is incorrect because these changes are completely natural during puberty. Everyone experiences them, though timing may differ. This belief can harm your self-confidence and create unnecessary worry. Proper knowledge helps you accept these changes positively instead of fearing them.

D. Hide and avoid everyone

Hiding and avoiding people may feel like a way to escape embarrassment, but it leads to isolation and emotional stress. It prevents you from seeking help or understanding what’s happening. Social connection and guidance are important during this phase, so avoiding others is not a healthy or helpful response.
