Safe & Unsafe Touch Awareness

You are in a familiar environment where a known adult, such as a family friend or relative, tries to hug you. However, the way they do it makes you feel uncomfortable and uneasy. You are confused because the person is known to you, and you wonder whether it is okay to react or not. You start feeling nervous and unsure about how to respond. No one else seems to notice your discomfort, and you feel stuck in that moment.

This situation highlights the importance of understanding personal safety and recognizing that even familiar people can sometimes cross boundaries unintentionally or inappropriately.

Review & Insights

B. Move away and tell a trusted adult

Moving away and telling a trusted adult is the best choice. It protects your safety and ensures that someone responsible is aware of the situation. Speaking up helps prevent future incidents and reinforces that your boundaries matter, even with people you know and trust.

A. Stay quiet because they are known

Staying quiet because the person is known is not safe. Familiarity does not make an uncomfortable touch acceptable. Ignoring your feelings can put you at risk and prevent others from helping you. Your comfort and safety always come first, regardless of who the person is.

C. Ignore and forget

Ignoring and forgetting may seem easier, but it does not address the problem. It can lead to repeated discomfort or unsafe situations. Understanding and acting on your feelings is important for your safety, so ignoring is not the right approach.

D. Smile and accept it

Smiling and accepting the situation ignores your discomfort and sends the wrong message. It allows the behavior to continue and may encourage similar actions in the future. Your feelings are valid, and you should never feel forced to accept something that makes you uncomfortable.
