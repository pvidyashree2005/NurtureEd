Hygiene & Self-Care Awareness

In a classroom, one student does not maintain basic hygiene, such as keeping clean clothes or proper personal care. Over time, some classmates begin to notice this and start making fun of the student. They laugh, whisper, and pass comments, which makes the student feel uncomfortable and isolated. The student becomes quieter and avoids interacting with others.

While a few students feel that the behavior is wrong, they are unsure whether to step in or stay silent. The situation highlights the importance of both personal hygiene and treating others with respect and kindness in a shared environment.

Review & Insights

C. Support respectfully and encourage good habits

Supporting the student respectfully and encouraging good habits is the best approach. It shows empathy and maturity while helping the student improve without feeling embarrassed. This approach promotes both personal hygiene awareness and a positive, inclusive environment where everyone feels respected and supported.

A. Laugh along with others

Laughing along may seem like fitting in with the group, but it hurts the student emotionally. It encourages bullying and creates a negative environment. Instead of helping, it lowers the student’s confidence and makes them feel excluded, which is not respectful or supportive behavior.

B. Ignore the situation

Ignoring the situation avoids direct involvement but does not stop the problem. Silence allows the teasing to continue and makes the affected student feel unsupported. Taking a positive stand is important to promote kindness and ensure a safe and respectful classroom for everyone.

D. Make jokes about it

Making jokes directly adds to the harm and embarrassment the student is already facing. It strengthens negative behavior and can deeply affect the student’s self-esteem. Instead of creating a friendly environment, it increases discomfort and discourages the student from improving or interacting with others.
